Portfolio — Case Study

Digital
Stone Age

A hypothetical Gen Z device detox app. The brief: if you already know you're addicted to your phone, what does it look like to make that fact measurable?

Project Overview

The starting point for Digital Stone Age was a behavioural observation rather than a product gap. Most digital wellness tools operate on disclosure: they show you, after the fact, how much time you spent and on what. The assumption is that awareness produces change.

The brief I set for this project was narrower: design something that doesn't inform you, it catches you. The concept is simple and a little absurd — you set a desired detox window, and if the phone is picked up or held at any point within it, the timer stops and shows you exactly how long your willpower lasted. A completely extreme idea, presented completely seriously.

The deliverables were a Google Play Store mockup, a brand identity with palette and logo, and a preview video.

Brand Identity

Colour Palette

The palette was built to feel like something physical and worn, not a tech product. Nothing here reads as a wellness app.

#D4732C Burnt sienna. The primary. Earthy, direct, tactile.
#FD9E5C Warm amber. Secondary. Approachable, faded heat.
#DEDDC6 Limestone. Background tone. Aged, quiet, grounded.

The Concept

What I was designing around

The Pomodoro technique exists to help people work in focused intervals. DSA borrows the structure and inverts the purpose. The app is not about productivity. The phone is not a tool you are being helped to use better. It is the thing you are trying to put down. That inversion needed to be felt in every design decision, including what the app does not do: it does not congratulate you, notify you, or ask you to share.

The mechanic

You set a focus duration, a break duration, and a number of cycles. During focus time, any touch, pick-up, or detected motion ends the round and logs exactly how far you got. During breaks the phone is yours.
