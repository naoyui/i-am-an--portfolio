Portfolio — Case Study

OFF/BEAT
Application Site

A single-page interactive website built to apply to a company that wouldn't say what they do.

Project Overview

OFF/BEAT launched with a website that revealed five fake products one click at a time before asking you to apply. The format was a personality test disguised as a product launch. It was also genuinely good brand writing.

The application form asked sharp questions. But a form answer sits in a spreadsheet. A website that mirrors the exact format, energy, and logic of the company you're applying to sits in a browser tab and keeps working.

This was built to answer one brief: what would an OFF/BEAT hiring page look like if the candidate made it instead of the company?

The Approach

Mirror the format, not the content

OFF/BEAT used fake product reveals to test whether someone had patience and curiosity. The response used the same mechanic to deliver real proof of work. Same structure, different direction.

Copy as strategy

Every bullet on every page is doing two things: making you smile and telling you something true. That's the job of brand copy. The site was an exercise in writing that earns its own attention.

No scroll, no noise

One viewport. One click at a time. The constraint was intentional. OFF/BEAT's site doesn't let you skim. Neither does this one.

The Site

Built as a standalone interactive page. Each click advances the reveal, the same way OFF/BEAT's product pages did. The writing carries the structure.
